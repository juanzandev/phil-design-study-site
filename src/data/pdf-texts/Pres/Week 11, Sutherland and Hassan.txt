are explicitly and consciously steered with the help of technology.
This reduction of human freedom was even perceived as a threat to
human dignity; if human actions are not a result of deliberate
decisions but of steering technologies, then people were
thought to be deprived of what makes them human. Moreover, if
they are not acting in freedom, their actions cannot be called moral.

Determinism
Determinism is the philosophical doctrine
that all events—including human actions,
choices, and cognition—are necessitated by
preceding causes, natural laws, and
environmental or genetic factors. It posits
that, given the state of the universe at any
moment, the future is fixed and
unavoidable, often considered incompatible
with free will.

Compatibilism
…or soft determinism, is the philosophical position
that free will and determinism are not mutually
exclusive, allowing both to coexist. It argues that
freedom is acting according to one’s motivations or
desires without external constraints, even if those
desires are determined by prior causes.
David Hume

Technological
Determinism
The theory that a society's technology
drives the development of its social
structure, culture, and history. It argues
that technological advancements possess
an internal logic, act as the primary
independent agent of social change, and
are often considered uncontrollable,
creating unstoppable, automatic effects
on human behavior and organization. Karl Marx

to us…
Technologies have to be interpreted and
appropriated by their users to be more than
just objects lying around. Only when
human beings use them, artifacts become
artifacts for doing something. And this "for
doing something" is determined not
entirely by the properties of the artifact
itself but also by the ways users deal with
them.

Design to Automata
In the first, that of the tool, the physical energy
necessary for labour and the required intellectual
input still depended upon the subject.
In the second, that of the machine, physical energy
becomes objectified by means of technique.
Finally, in the third stage, that of automata, technical
means make dispensable input from the subject.
