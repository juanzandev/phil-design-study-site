Formalism is a philosophical position that emphasizes
form over meaning. A formalist’s approach in
aesthetics argues that all you need to make an
assessment is what is formally presented to you. It is
not necessary to place any conceptual understanding
about the object, historical or social background, onto
your experience. You simply attend to the form and
place judgment through that alone.

The intellect keeps trying to assign a conceptual
understanding to the representation. But it cannot
settle on which conceptualization is right, because
there is no correct assignment of concepts. This
leads to free play between understanding and the
imagination. “The power of cognition that are set
into play by this representation are hereby in a free
play, since no determinate concetpts restricts them
to a particular rule of cognition” (5:217). The free
play of understanding and imagination give rise to
pleasure, and that is the experience of beauty. The
judgment arises as a result of the inability to fix
concepts.

27
-- 27 of 41 --
post-Kantian idealism,
The goal of architecture is by no means simply to combine utility with
beauty, but is rather to express
some idea or ideas, with functionality being
at most a necessary condition but not part of the achievement of the goal
of the art and with beauty itself being redefined as expression rather than
anything more formal.
- Schelling the thought is that a building should express its own function.
- Schopenhauer that it should express the nature of its own construction
and the physical forces on which that depends.
- Hegel that it should express metaphysical ideas about divinity and
spirit itself.
