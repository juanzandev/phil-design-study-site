Scaffold Theory..
According to extended theories
of cognition and affect,
scaffolds are processes and
artifacts used to express,
trigger, intensify or modulate
emotions or cognitive states.

Cognitive Scaffold
Web applications, including social media
platforms, should rather be seen as
cognitive and memory scaffolds. This is
because social media platforms aid in
cognitive and memory tasks, such as
accessing information, or reminding users
of different events.

Social Media Platforms
as Attentional Scaffolds
Every element of the user interface of social
media platforms is chosen because it works
to attract and capture people’s
attention–for example, ‘pull-to-refresh’
mechanisms in newsfeeds and infinite
scrolling feeds are implemented in almost all
social media platforms because of their
potential to maximize time spent online

Beneficial
Scaffolds
Beneficial scaffolds are
generally those that are created
to help users with various tasks
and to advance their interests.
Hostile Scaffold
hostile scaffolds undermine the
interests of the agent using the
scaffold, while at the same time,
serving the interests of another
agent, usually the one who
created it

intermittent
rewards
In behavioral sciences and psychology rewards refer to any positive
reinforcement that encourages specific behaviors and feelings,
shaping choices and learning. Psychologists argue that intermittent
rewards can be more effective than predictable rewards in
establishing habits, regardless of overall gain or loss. This is because
intermittent rewards lead to the release of dopamine which ends up
reinforcing the behavior that generates the intermittent rewards
